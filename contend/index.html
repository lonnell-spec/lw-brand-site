// Marketing Assessment — Campaign Direction Review
✓ Strengths

Platform timing is exceptional. An 80K-attended event landing on release week is not a marketing tactic — it’s a distribution event. Contend Sunday (200+ churches) creates a synchronized, organic word-of-mouth engine that no budget can replicate.

⚠ Gaps to Close

Email is the single largest risk. Building 50K from zero by October is aggressive. Start pixel capture on the current site immediately. The email runway shrinks every week it’s delayed. Platform is a megaphone; email is a conversion tool.

✓ Direction Verdict

This is going in the right direction. The ACCESS Dallas anchor, Contend Sunday, multi-platform influencer activation, and tiered pre-order incentives are structurally sound. The missing piece isn’t strategy — it’s speed.

// 01 — Core Audience
WHO THIS IS FOR
Primary Reader

The Frustrated Faithful

A Christian between 18 and 40 who loves Jesus but is frustrated with the state of the modern church. Spiritually hungry. Drawn to authenticity over aesthetics.

The 2819 Church congregation (6,000+ weekly) and Pastor Philip’s social media audience (5.2M+) skew heavily toward this demographic — Black and multicultural audiences, urban professionals, and college students.

Secondary — Ministry

Pastors & Leaders

Leaders looking for language and conviction to preach boldly. This tier drives bulk orders.

Secondary — Seekers

Spiritually Curious Non-Believers

Gen Z and Millennials who have rejected institutional religion but are open to encountering the real Jesus.

Secondary — Family

Praying Parents

Parents praying for their children’s faith. Gift purchasers and multipliers.

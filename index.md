## What I Carry (descriptions, not aspirations)

The five things I've actually been hired, called, and trusted to do — across corporate sales floors, seminary classrooms, and ministry organizations.

1. **Vision without execution is a daydream.** If your org has a clear vision and broken infrastructure — that's the gap. The operator's job is to close it.
2. **The architect who sees frameworks where others see chaos.** If you're scaling and it's starting to break — that's not a growth problem, it's a systems problem. Systems are buildable.
3. **Weight was made to be carried.** If you're the one carrying what no one else will touch — you weren't made for comfort. You were made for this.
4. **Words build worlds.** If your culture isn't moving the direction you want — your language is building something you didn't intend. Words are infrastructure too.
5. **Assigned, not wandering.** If the weight feels unbearable, it's often because the calling isn't clear. Clarity doesn't remove the weight; it makes it worth carrying.

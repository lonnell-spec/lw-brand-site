02 · The Anchor

Growth Revealed
A Fracture.

Acts 6. The church is exploding. Three thousand saved. Five thousand more. The system built for 120 can't hold 5,000 — and the widows fall through the cracks. Every XP in this room has a widow problem right now. You know what yours is.

— The Text

"Now in these days when the disciples were increasing in number, a complaint by the Hellenists arose against the Hebrews because their widows were being neglected in the daily distribution."— Acts 6:1

"It is not right that we should give up preaching the word of God to serve tables… we will devote ourselves to prayer and to the ministry of the word."— Acts 6:2, 4

The Greek word for serve tables is diakonein. The Greek word for ministry of the word is diakonia. Same root. Luke isn't making a distinction of value. He's making a distinction of assignment.

The spreadsheet is ministry. Luke said so first.
— The Deeper Word

The apostles weren't protecting their schedule. They weren't protecting their preference. They were protecting their oxygen. They built external structure so they could protect internal oxygen.

Acts 6:7 — "the word of God continued to increase, and the disciples multiplied greatly, and a great many of the priests became obedient to the faith."

When you fix the plumbing, the house doesn't shrink. It expands.
03 · System One · External

The Decision Matrix.

Saves your Senior Pastor's bandwidth. Stops every decision from landing on their desk. Four tiers. Written. Shared. Audited.

— The Story

Two years in, I was managing every decision at 2819. Every hire. Every budget line. Every Sunday adjustment. I told myself I was being helpful. I was the bottleneck.

The night I realized it, I couldn't sleep. 3 AM. I wrote every decision I'd made that week on one sheet of paper. Forty-seven decisions in one week. How many actually required me? Six.

— The Theology · Exodus 18

"What you are doing is not good. You will surely wear yourself out, both you and these people with you. For the thing is too heavy for you. You are not able to do it alone."— Exodus 18:17–18

God gave Moses the first org chart in scripture through a Midianite operations consultant. Not a prophet. A father-in-law who watched Moses bottleneck and said — build tiers.

04 · System Two · External

Protect. Produce. Prepare.

Saves your week. Three lanes. Ten minutes every Sunday. Paper, not phone. Shared with your spouse and every direct report.

— The Story

Fourteen months in. A week where I produced everything and protected nothing. Three sermons. A budget revision. A staffing proposal. A run-of-show. A donor comm.

Saturday night, my wife asked when we'd last had a real conversation. I couldn't remember. I had produced at a high level — and protected nothing. Not my marriage. Not time with God. Not my children. Not my health.

The church is never going to be easier. That's when I built this.

— The Theology · Nehemiah

"Those who carried burdens were loaded in such a way that each labored on the work with one hand and held his weapon with the other."— Nehemiah 4:17

One hand builds. One hand guards. And throughout the book, Nehemiah is praying. The work doesn't replace the rhythm. The work is inside the rhythm.

— The System · Three Lanes
Protect
What am I protecting this week? Marriage. Key staff. My engine. Time with God. Culture.
Only Protect
= You Become HR.
You Stop Leading.
Produce
What am I producing this week? Sunday. The things only I can execute.
Only Produce
= Burnout In 18 Months.
The Default Killer.
Prepare
What am I preparing for 90 days out? Next hire. Next campus. Next season.
Only Prepare
= Nothing Ships.
Team Loses Confidence.

If your spouse cannot name what you are protecting this week, you are not protecting it.
05 · System Three · Internal

The Engine Statement.

Saves your soul. Three sentences. Pinned where you see them every day. Names what only you can do — before you delegate it without noticing.

— The Story

I built the first two a year before I built this one. The Matrix freed my time. The Grid structured my week. I had more bandwidth than I'd had in eighteen months — and I used it to pick up new things. Every gap. Every underperforming area.

I had built systems to stop being the bottleneck. I used the freed capacity to become a different kind of bottleneck — one that looked like leadership and was actually drift. I knew my job description. I didn't know my engine.

— The Theology · Mark 1:35

"And rising very early in the morning, while it was still dark, he departed and went out to a desolate place, and there he prayed."— Mark 1:35

The crowds were back looking for him. The disciples said "Everyone is looking for you." Jesus said "Let us go on to the next towns, that I may preach there also, for that is why I came out." Jesus named his engine in one sentence.

— The System · Three Sentences

Write three sentences. Not tasks. Postures, relationships, inputs. Your prayer and word. Your elder shepherding. Your marriage. Your time with your children. The parts that — if you stopped — would fail slow, invisibly, over months. By the time you noticed, you'd be three years into a drift you can't reverse in three weeks.

— Do This Now · In The Room
Only I Can              .
Phone out. Or paper. Write one sentence. Not three. One. Rule: put something in that blank that is NOT a task. Sixty seconds.
  • Only I can carry the theological weight of what this church preaches.
  • Only I can protect the marriage of my senior pastor by making sure our staff culture doesn't drain him.
  • Only I can be the one who asks my daughter how her heart is when I pick her up from school.
If you don't name your engine, you will delegate it without noticing.
— The Loop · All Three Together

The three systems work as a loop. Pull one out and the other two start drifting. Install all three and you have something that will hold the church you're building and the minister you're becoming — for the long haul.

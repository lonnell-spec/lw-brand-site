02 · The Anchor

Acts 6. The First
Executive Council.

The church is exploding. Three thousand saved. Five thousand more. The system built for 120 can't hold the growth — and the widows fall through the cracks. The apostles didn't pray harder. They didn't preach louder. They built.

— The Text

"Now in these days when the disciples were increasing in number, a complaint by the Hellenists arose against the Hebrews because their widows were being neglected in the daily distribution."— Acts 6:1

"It is not right that we should give up preaching the word of God to serve tables… we will devote ourselves to prayer and to the ministry of the word."— Acts 6:2, 4

The Greek word for serve tables is diakonein. The Greek word for ministry of the word is diakonia. Same root. Luke isn't making a distinction of value. He's making a distinction of assignment. The spreadsheet is ministry. Luke said so first.

— Ask Yourself · Right Now
What widow is being              right now?
Not hypothetical. The complaint you've heard three times this month and been managing instead of analyzing. The group, the ministry, the person falling through. Name her. Sixty seconds.
The complaint is the smoke. Your job is to find the fire.
03 · The Six A's · Part One

Analyze. Assess. Assign.

The apostles didn't skip to the solution. They walked a process. Six moves, in order. Miss one and the fix doesn't hold. These are the first three — what happens before you ever name a name.

— A1 · Analyze · Acts 6:1 · The Complaint

"A complaint by the Hellenists arose against the Hebrews because their widows were being neglected in the daily distribution."— Acts 6:1

Ask yourself: "What complaint have I heard three times this month that I've been managing instead of analyzing?"

The handle. One hour. Alone. Blank sheet. Every complaint from the last 90 days. Circle what repeats.

— A2 · Assess · Acts 6:2 · Name Your Oxygen

"It is not right that we should give up preaching the word of God to serve tables."— Acts 6:2

Prayer and the word weren't the apostles' deliverable. They were their oxygen. Before they delegated anything, they named what they could not delegate.

Ask yourself: "If I could only do three things in my ministry this year — what would they be?"

The handle. Tonight: write three sentences. "Only I can do ______." Pin them where you see them daily.

— A3 · Assign · Acts 6:3 · Three Qualifications

"Pick out from among you seven men of good repute, full of the Spirit and of wisdom, whom we will appoint to this duty."— Acts 6:3

Three qualifications. Reputation. Spirit. Wisdom. Availability isn't on the list. Availability is not a qualification. Multiplication is.

Ask yourself: "The last person I delegated to — did I pick for availability, or for the three qualifications?"

The handle. Before the next hire: reputation, Spirit, wisdom. All yes — or the role stays open.

Availability is not a qualification. Multiplication is.
04 · The Six A's · Part Two

Attest. Affirm. Acknowledge.

Most leaders stop at assign. The apostles didn't. Three more moves separate silent confusion from covenant. Miss these and the widow still doesn't eat.

— A4 · Attest · Acts 6:2,6 · Commission Publicly

"These they set before the apostles, and they prayed and laid their hands on them."— Acts 6:6

The whole community. Prayer. Laid hands. Public transfer of authority. Private delegation creates silent confusion. Public commissioning creates covenant.

Ask yourself: "The last three I delegated to — does the staff know publicly that they own that ministry?"

The handle. Next delegation: staff meeting or Sunday. Pray. Lay hands. Name the authority transfer.

— A5 · Affirm · Acts 6:5 · Trusted By The Affected

"And what they said pleased the whole gathering."— Acts 6:5

Read the names in Acts 6:5. They're all Greek. The apostles picked Hellenists to care for Hellenist widows. They didn't pick the most available. They picked people the affected already trusted.

Ask yourself: "The person I assigned — do the people this ministry serves actually trust them?"

The handle. Before you assign, ask three of the affected who they trust. Fifteen minutes saves six months.

— A6 · Acknowledge · Acts 6:7 · Measure The Fruit

"And the word of God continued to increase, and the number of the disciples multiplied greatly in Jerusalem."— Acts 6:7

Verse 7 is the scoreboard. The fix worked. The church grew. The widows ate. Not "they were busy." The widow got fed.

Ask yourself: "The last delegation — do I know whether it worked? Not whether they're busy. Whether the widow got fed."

The handle. Every delegation gets a 90-day acknowledge meeting. Non-movable.

Private delegation creates silent confusion. Public commissioning creates covenant.
05 · The Three Truths

Fix The Plumbing.
The House Expands.

What the Six A's actually protect. What they actually produce. Why the apostles bothered. Three truths that sit underneath every move.

— Truth 01 · The Spreadsheet Is Ministry

Diakonein. Diakonia. Same root. The table and the text carry the same weight in Luke's Greek. Management isn't less spiritual than preaching. It's the other half of the same word. If it keeps the widow fed, it's ministry.

— Truth 02 · Structure Is How Spirit Scales

Acts 6:7 only happens because Acts 6:3–6 happened. The word multiplied because the structure held. Structure is not the opposite of Spirit. Structure is how Spirit scales. Manage the church so you can stay a minister.

— Truth 03 · The Widow Gets Fed

Every metric in Acts 6 traces back to one question — did the widow eat? Not attendance. Not budget. Not platform. Did the person being neglected get served? That's the only scoreboard that matters. Every A points at her.

Manage the church so you can stay a minister.

CRITICAL FIXES
01 — DECLARE THE HIERARCHY

Builder is the spine. Author and Speaker are downstream expressions. The hero section, bio, and speaking page need to reflect this order. Update the hero copy to lead with Builder as the primary identity. One sentence: "I build things — churches, systems, books, and leaders."

02 — PUT THE ORIGIN STORY BACK

Cisco. Splunk. Enterprise sales. This is the differentiator that makes the builder identity credible — not just aspirational. Add it to the bio, about page, and every speaking pitch. Write it in your voice, not LinkedIn voice.

03 — CLOSE THE VOICE GAP

Confrontational in the pulpit. Careful on the page. The confrontational register is the brand. Add one rule: if a first-year seminary student could have written this, rewrite it.

04 — BUILD THE AENA → ZAG BRIDGE

AENA is the prerequisite for ZAG. Make this the stated sequence publicly. Two books in sequence = a curriculum = a course = a business. Write one essay or section making this argument explicitly.

05 — NAME THE ENEMY  ✓ PARTIAL FIX LIVE

Hero tagline now reads: "Most organizations don't fail from too little vision. They fail from too little infrastructure." Enemy is named. Extend this language to the about section and speaking page to reinforce across more surfaces.

06 — SURFACE 2819 AS LIVE PROOF  ✓ FIX LIVE

About bio now reads: "scaled 2819 Church in Atlanta from 13 to 50 staff members in six months" — organization named explicitly, proof is verifiable. Next: link 2819 Church name to 2819church.org for full credibility stack.

The One Metric That Tells You It's Working
NEWSLETTER SUBSCRIBER GROWTH RATE

Not likes. Not followers. Not views. Every subscriber is a potential speaking booking, consulting client, course buyer, and book pre-order. If the list is growing week over week — the strategy is working. If it is flat, something in the funnel is broken.

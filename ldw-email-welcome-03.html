%FIRSTNAME%, there's a tension every faith leader carries quietly.

The guilt of opening a spreadsheet before opening your Bible. The unspoken question of whether trusting data means not trusting God. The feeling that using operational tools is somehow a concession — a sign that faith alone wasn't enough.

I want to name it and close it. Because it is the wrong question.

Here's what I actually believe:

THE OPERATING BELIEF
"The Holy Spirit is the preeminent decision maker. Data helps inform — it does not replace."

That's not a tagline. It's the operating principle that makes everything else I do coherent.

A steward is responsible for what they've been given. A leader who makes major organizational decisions without the information available to them is not exercising faith — they're exercising negligence.

The Spirit leads. Data removes the noise so the signal is cleaner. The dashboard doesn't make the call. It clears the table so the right voice can be heard.

This is why I build. Not because technology is the answer. Because leaders who are serious about their calling are obligated to be serious about their craft. Both hands. The pastoral and the operational. The Spirit and the system.

That's the tension this newsletter exists to resolve.

Informed leaders make better decisions.
That's not a lack of faith — it's stewardship.
